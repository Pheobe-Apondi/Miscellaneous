UX Research

This class is all about research. I know when you come across the word "research", all you can picture is a lot of paper work. I promise you, there's more to it. In this class we try to understand users' needs, wants, behaviours and painpoints.

To be able to create a good tech product, it is essential that we understand the end users of our product. This results in us creating a product users want and also saving money. These are some of the things I have learn't so far:

  • Our products should be a human centred design, meaning we should keep our users in mind when building the product.
  • There are two methods of research, primary and secondary method. In primary we collect data ourselves and in secondary we get it from another source.
  • There are different types of research e.g descriptive and evaluative.
  • There are different data collection methods e.g observation and interviews
  • The best approach to research is to use the two methods of research i.e primary and secondary.

UI/UX Design

In this class it is all about the visual representation of a product and how that product makes the users feel. It is still human centered. We focus on creating visual elements that will appeal to the user of a tech product and also ensuring that the user has a good experience while using the application. Here are some of the things I have learnt.
  • There are different elements of design e.g shapes, colors and textures
  • Design has different principles that one must follow e.g balance and dominance
  • You don't need to be an artist to be a designers, designers solve problems
  • Mortifs: These are designs that can be recreated. They can be repeated to create a pattern
  • There are design tools e.g Figma that are used to create designs
